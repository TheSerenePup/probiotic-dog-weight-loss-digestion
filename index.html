2. Why this hidden cause happens (medical reasons for dog weight gain)

Gut dysbiosis — an imbalance of good and bad bacteria — is common in adult dogs, especially after antibiotics, stress, or age. When the microbiome is off, inflammation rises and metabolism slows. Even thyroid issues or steroid medications amplify this effect. The gut becomes an “overachiever” at absorbing every last calorie. And because it’s invisible, owners keep cutting food … while the dog’s body holds onto weight.

6. How to choose the right probiotic (vet-backed criteria)

Not all probiotics are equal. Before you buy, use this simple framework:

  • Prebiotics included: Inulin or FOS feed the good bacteria.
  • No unnecessary fillers: Avoid artificial colors or sugars.
